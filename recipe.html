pineapple mint soda

submitted by John D.
****
You've probably tasted pineapple soda, but did you know that you can make it yourself? We're going to make pineapple soda with a touch of mint just for added redreshment. Follow the directions below, sit under the nearest palm tree and feel the tropical vibe envelop your body.

ingredients

  1. 7 cups pineapple chunks
  2. 1 cup brown sugar
  3. 2 cups white sugar
  4. 1 bunch fresh mint, washed
  5. 2 tbsp lemon juice
  6. carbonated water, to serve

instructions

  1. Combine the pineapple and both sugars in a large, covered pot, stirring occasionally, for 12-24 hours. The fruit will shrink and release its juice and the sugar will dissolve.
  2. Tie mint together at the stems with kitchen string and dunk the leaves into the fruit mixture. Cover the pot and bring to a gentle boil over high heat, then lower the heat and simmer for 3 minutes. Turn off the heat and let the mixture steep, covered, for 30 minutes.
  3. Remove the mint and squeeze out as much of its liquid as you can back in the pot. Strain the pineapple from the liquid and remove. Stir in the lemon juice.
  4. Mix the syrup with carbonated water in a 1:2 ratio. Serve with ice.
